Privacy Policy

We respect your privacy. This policy explains what personal information we collect, why we collect it, and how you can contact us about your data.

1. Who we are

Foursquare Gospel Church — Ijoko Missionary District Headquarters ("we", "us", "the church"). Our address: Ijoko, Ogun State, Nigeria. For questions about this policy, email privacy@foursquare-ijoko.example or use our contact page.

2. Scope

This policy applies to personal information collected through our website (all pages under this domain), forms (contact, event RSVPs, ministry signup, donation forms), in-person activities, and communications with our staff or volunteers.

3. Information we collect

  • Contact details: name, email, phone, postal address.
  • Membership & ministry information: ministry interests, volunteering history, service attendance notes.
  • Event information: RSVPs, dietary requirements, special needs.
  • Donation information: transaction records (amount, date, payment method). We do not store full payment card numbers on our site — third-party payment processors handle card data.
  • Communications: messages you send us, and any attachments (e.g. transfer receipts).
  • Technical data: IP address, browser type, device information, cookies and analytics data collected automatically when you visit the site.

4. How we use your information

We use personal information to:

  • Respond to enquiries and provide services, event administration, and pastoral support.
  • Manage membership, ministry participation, volunteer coordination and safeguarding where required.
  • Process donations and issue receipts.
  • Send newsletters, event invitations and important church announcements (we will only email you if you opt-in).
  • Comply with legal obligations and protect the safety of individuals.

5. Legal basis and legitimate interests

Where applicable (for residents of jurisdictions with data protection laws), we process personal data based on:

  • Consent — for mailings and optional services you agree to.
  • Contractual necessity — to provide services you requested (e.g., event registration, donations).
  • Legal obligations — where we must retain records for accounting or safeguarding purposes.
  • Legitimate interests — running church activities and administration in a way that does not unduly affect your rights.

6. Cookies & tracking

We use cookies and similar technologies to make the site work and to understand how visitors use it. This may include:

  • Essential cookies required for site functionality.
  • Performance and analytics cookies (e.g., Google Analytics) to understand traffic patterns. These are typically aggregated and anonymised.

You can control cookies through your browser settings. If you block all cookies some parts of the site may not work.

7. Third-party services

We use third-party services for functionality including:

  • Payment processors (e.g., Paystack, Flutterwave, PayPal) to process online donations. These providers have their own privacy policies.
  • Embedding services (e.g., Google Maps, YouTube) to show maps and videos.
  • Analytics providers (e.g., Google Analytics) to measure and improve the site.

We do not sell your personal data to third parties.

8. Donations & payment data

When you donate online we collect the information necessary to process the donation and issue receipts. Payment card details are handled by the payment provider and are not stored on our servers. We retain transactional records (payer name, amount, date, purpose) for accounting and audit purposes.

9. Sharing and disclosure

We may share data:

  • With trusted third parties who provide services to the church (payment providers, mailing services, IT providers) under contract and with appropriate safeguards.
  • Where required by law, court order, or safeguarding investigations.

10. Data security

We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect personal information. However, no transmission over the internet or storage method is completely secure — please contact us if you have concerns.

11. Data retention

We retain personal data only as long as necessary for the purposes described or to comply with legal obligations. Typical retention examples:

  • Donation records: retained for at least the period required by accounting laws (e.g., 5–7 years, adjust to local requirements).
  • Contact/enquiry messages: retained until the matter is resolved, unless you ask for deletion.

If you want a copy of retention schedules for specific data types, contact privacy@foursquare-ijoko.example.

12. Your rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Request correction of inaccurate data.
  • Request deletion (subject to legal or contractual retention obligations).
  • Restrict or object to processing in certain circumstances.
  • Request portability of your data in a machine
